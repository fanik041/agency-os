# Cold Calling Script & Website Audit Guide

## Your Calling Script

### OPENING (15-30 seconds)
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company].
I've been researching [Industry/Company Type] and came across [Company Name].
I noticed a few opportunities where you might be leaving money on the table.
Do you have 60 seconds?"
```

**If they say NO:** "Would Thursday or Friday afternoon work better?"
**If they say YES:** Move to Discovery

---

## DISCOVERY QUESTION (Ask the most relevant one)

Choose ONE main pain point based on what you observed on their website:

### 🔍 If SEO Score is LOW (0-5):
> "I noticed you're not showing up much in Google for [key industry keywords].
> How much of your new business comes from organic search right now?"

**Why this works:** It's specific, based on research, and gives them a chance to admit the gap.

### 💬 If Lead Capture Score is LOW (0-4):
> "I was on your website and noticed you're getting traffic, but I didn't see
> an obvious way for visitors to stay connected with you. What happens when
> someone leaves your site interested but not ready to buy?"

**Why this works:** You've shown you looked at their site, and you're asking about a real problem.

### 💰 If Revenue Opportunity is HIGH (5-7):
> "You're doing [current revenue source] well, but I noticed you're missing out on
> [adjacent revenue stream]. Are you capturing any revenue from [X]?"

**Why this works:** You're positioning yourself as someone who understands their business deeply.

---

## POSITIONING (30 seconds)
```
"The businesses I work with typically fall into one of three buckets:

1) They're not getting found online — traffic problem
2) They're getting traffic but not capturing leads — conversion problem
3) They have customers but aren't maximizing what they spend — monetization problem

Based on what I see, it looks like you're dealing with [#1/2/3]."
```

This positions you as consultative, not salesy.

---

## SOFT CLOSE (Soft, not hard sell)
```
"I'd love to spend 15 minutes looking a bit deeper at [Company]
and walk you through exactly what we've found.

Would Tuesday or Wednesday afternoon work better for a quick call?"
```

**Key:** You're not asking for the sale, you're asking for information. This feels natural and non-threatening.

---

## KEY PHRASES TO USE
- ✓ "I was researching..."
- ✓ "I noticed..."
- ✓ "A lot of [business type] I work with..."
- ✓ "What's your biggest bottleneck right now?"
- ✓ "How would [specific outcome] impact your business?"
- ✓ "Can I show you what we've found?"

---

## WHAT NOT TO SAY ❌
- ❌ "We can help with everything"
- ❌ "Our service is the best in the industry"
- ❌ "Can I schedule you for a demo?"
- ❌ "We've worked with companies like [your competitor]"
- ❌ "You really need to do something about [X]" (too aggressive)
- ❌ "A lot of businesses fail because..." (fear-based)

---

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "I'm not interested"
> "I understand — you might not be. I just wanted to show you what I found.
> Is there a better time to catch you? I'm pretty flexible."

### "We already have someone doing that"
> "Got it. Quick question — how's that working out? [Listen]
> The reason I ask is I often find businesses have gaps even with existing solutions."

### "We don't have budget right now"
> "I hear that a lot. Here's what I usually suggest —
> let me show you what this could mean for your [revenue/leads/visibility],
> and then you can decide if it's worth budgeting for. Cool?"

### "Can you send me something?"
> "Absolutely, I can — but honestly, a 15-minute call will give me a clearer picture
> of your specific situation. That way I can send something actually relevant.
> Would Tuesday work?"

## WEBSITE AUDIT CHECKLIST

### SEO AUDIT (Quick Check) — Score: 0-8

Check for each item (1 point each):
- [ ] Title tags present and keyword-relevant
- [ ] Meta descriptions present and compelling
- [ ] H1 tags clear, keyword-aligned, appear on page
- [ ] Internal linking structure visible (footer, nav, body links)
- [ ] Mobile-friendly design (responsive, not squished)
- [ ] Page speed acceptable (loads in <3 seconds)
- [ ] Schema markup implemented (check source code)
- [ ] Google Business Profile complete and optimized

**SEO Score: ___ / 8**

---

### LEAD RETENTION & CONVERSION — Score: 0-7

Check for each item (1 point each):
- [ ] Email capture/newsletter signup present (above fold or prominent)
- [ ] Chatbot, contact form, or "Request Demo" visible
- [ ] Primary CTA button clear (obvious what to do next)
- [ ] Exit-intent popup or offer (fires when leaving)
- [ ] Live chat, phone number, or contact options visible
- [ ] Social media links present and functional
- [ ] Multiple ways to contact (email, phone, form, social)

**Lead Capture Score: ___ / 7**

---

### REVENUE OPPORTUNITY GAPS — Score: 0-7

Check what's MISSING (more gaps = higher score = more opportunity):
- [ ] No complementary product/service offered
- [ ] No upsell strategy visible (one price tier only)
- [ ] Missing premium/VIP tier
- [ ] No membership or subscription option
- [ ] No bundle deals or packages
- [ ] Limited payment options (no recurring billing)
- [ ] No FAQ, case studies, or social proof to reduce friction

**Revenue Opportunity Score: ___ / 7**
(Higher score = more opportunity to create revenue solutions)

## HOW TO USE WITH THE SPREADSHEET

1. **Before calling:** Visit their website and fill in the three score columns (SEO, Lead Capture, Revenue Opportunity)
2. **Total Score & Rank:** These calculate automatically — highest total = highest priority
3. **Sort by Priority Rank:** Call #1 priority leads first
4. **Track calls:** Fill in "Date Called" and "Call Script Used" after each call
5. **Take notes:** Brief notes on objections, interest level, next steps

## QUICK REFERENCE: WHAT TO SAY BY SITUATION

**High SEO Score + Low Lead Capture:**
> "Your site's getting found, but you're not capturing emails.
> I see an easy win here..."

**Low SEO + High Lead Capture:**
> "You're good at converting visitors, but not many are finding you.
> Let's fix the traffic piece first..."

**Low Revenue Opportunity (already monetized):**
> "Your business model is solid, but there's white space in [adjacent area].
> Here's how we'd approach it..."

---

## CALL STRUCTURE (Ideal Script Flow)

1. **OPENING** (15 sec) — Name, company, why you're calling
2. **DISCOVERY** (30 sec) — Ask ONE relevant question based on their site
3. **POSITIONING** (30 sec) — Show you understand their problem category
4. **SOFT CLOSE** (15 sec) — Ask for 15 min conversation, not a demo

**Total time: ~90 seconds**

---

## SUCCESS METRICS

Track these to refine your approach:
- **Call-through rate:** % of leads who actually take your call
- **Meeting rate:** % of calls that convert to meetings
- **Close rate:** % of meetings that become clients

Aim for: 40%+ call-through, 30%+ meeting rate, 20%+ close rate

If rates are low, adjust your opening and discovery questions.
